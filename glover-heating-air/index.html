Our Story

A Family Built on Showing Up

Glover Heating & Air Conditioning is owned and operated by Ralph Glover, who spent 30 years as a firefighter before turning that same discipline toward heating and cooling.

Ralph served with Center Point Fire, North Shelby Fire, and finished his 30-year fire and rescue career at Odenville Fire & Rescue. That's three decades of running toward calls when people needed help most — and it's the same standard he holds his HVAC work to today: show up, do the job right, and treat people the way you'd want your own family treated.

The family's commitment to service didn't stop with Ralph. His son, Eric, is also part of Glover Heating & Air Conditioning, and continues the family's fire service tradition as an active firefighter with Birmingham Fire and Rescue and Odenville Fire and Rescue. It's a family that has spent a combined lifetime running toward the calls other people run from — which is exactly the kind of reliability you get when Glover Heating & Air Conditioning answers the phone.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my AC needs a repair or a full replacement?

Age, repair frequency, and cost are the three things to weigh. If a unit is over 12–15 years old, needs frequent repairs, or a single repair costs close to half the price of a new system, replacement is usually the smarter long-term investment. Ralph will give you a straight answer either way — not just the one that costs more.

How often should I service my heating and air conditioning system?

Most systems in Central Alabama benefit from a check-up twice a year — once before summer cooling season and once before winter heating season. Regular maintenance catches small issues before they become expensive repairs.

Do you handle both heating and cooling systems?

Yes. Glover Heating & Air Conditioning services and installs both sides of the system — central air conditioners, heat pumps, and furnaces — so you only need one call for the whole system.

What areas do you serve?

Birmingham and the surrounding communities, including Center Point, Trussville, Clay, Springville, Odenville, Moody, Argo, Pell City, Chelsea, Pelham, and North Shelby County.

How long does a new HVAC system installation take?

A straightforward system change-out is typically completed in a single day. More involved installs — new ductwork, new construction, or larger systems — can take longer. Ralph will walk you through the timeline before any work starts.

How do I schedule a service call?

Call or text Ralph Glover directly at (205) 296-8029. You'll talk to the owner, not a call center.
