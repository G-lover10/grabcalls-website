🍳 Meal Ideas (lunch & dinner)

Tried-and-true firehouse meals — hearty, one-pot, pause-friendly when the tones drop. Tap a card to open the recipe.

Lunch · quick & easy

🥪 Sausage, Peppers & Onions Subs
One pan, ~30 min. Italian sausage + bell peppers + onions on hoagies w/ provolone. Cheap, fast, feeds 6 easy.
🌶️ Firehouse Chili
Hamburger + beans + chunky tomatoes + chili powder. Simmers while you run calls. Better the longer it sits.
🥘 Fire Station Casserole
Ground beef + tater tots + cream of mushroom + cheddar. Slow-cooker classic. Doubles to feed 10.
🌯 Sheet Pan Fajitas
Chicken or steak + peppers + onions, all on one tray at 425°F. Tortillas, salsa, sour cream on the side.
🥩 Italian Beef Sandwiches (crockpot)
Chuck roast + pepperoncini + Italian dressing mix. Start in the morning, slice and pile on hoagies for lunch.
🥔 Loaded Baked Potato Bar
Bake russets in the oven, set out chili / cheese / bacon / scallions / sour cream. Cheap, customizable, no two plates alike.

Dinner · hearty & filling

🍝 Classic Baked Lasagna
Make-ahead friendly. Pull from the oven anytime — reheats great if a call drags. Garlic bread on the side.
🍖 Beef Stew (or Guinness Stew)
Chuck roast + potatoes + carrots + onion + stock. Slow simmer all afternoon — Guinness version is a Milwaukee firehouse staple.
🐖 Pulled Pork
Pork shoulder + dry rub overnight, low & slow on smoker or crockpot. Buns, slaw, baked beans = full plate.
🍤 Jambalaya
One pot. Andouille + chicken + shrimp + rice + Cajun spice. Spicy, scales to 8 without breaking a sweat.
🍝 Spaghetti & Meatballs
Eternal crew favorite. Big pot of sauce, frozen meatballs if you're short on time. Garlic bread is mandatory.
🔥 Smoked Brisket
Set on the smoker at sunrise, eat at dinner. Mac & cheese, beans, white bread = Texas style.

💡 Firehouse rules: anything in a crockpot or smoker is gold — it can simmer while you run calls. One-pot & sheet-pan meals minimize cleanup. Always cook for one or two extra in case of detail-ins.

Behavior

How fairness works: Each shift, the rotation picks whoever's present with the lowest count. Detailed-off members are skipped automatically (count doesn't move → catches up naturally on return). Lt. Martin and Sgt. Glover are exempt.

Tiebreaker chain (when two crew are tied at lowest count):

  1. Detail break: if one of the tied crew was detailed off last shift, they lose the tie (give them a breather).
  2. Medic break (watch/backup only): medics on Rescue lose ties — they're acting officer, so the non-medic takes it.
  3. Newest first: if still tied, newest member takes it (Smart → Wilson → … → Andrews).

Backup Watch: Always from the opposite rig of primary. Uses today's lineup, so if you put Bentley on Engine for the night, the algorithm respects that.

Meals: Designated cook (Holt) auto-picks when present. When Holt's off, fair rotation among others.
